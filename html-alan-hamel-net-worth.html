    Alan Hamel Wiki Biography

    Alan Hamel, born on 30 June 1936 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, of Jewish descent, is a television host, producer, and entertainer, best known for co-hosting numerous television shows, including “Razzle Dazzle” and “Anniversary Game”. He’s also done commercial pitches and advertisements for various companies, which have significantly contributed to his net worth.

    How rich is Alan Hamel? As of early-2017, sources estimate a net worth that is at $100 million, mostly earned through a successful career in production, hosting, and business. He occasionally pursued acting and also engaged in production work. Additionally, he created several businesses, all of which have continued to bolster his wealth.

    Alan Hamel Net Worth $100 million

    Hamel attended Ryerson Institute of Technology in 1954 under the television arts program. Although he did not complete his studies, he found work as a weekend announcer with CKVR-TV and CKBB Radio. His career took off when he co-hosted the children’s television series entitled “Razzle Dazzle”, marking the beginning of the increase in his net worth.

    Alan subsequently transitioned to syndicated game shows, including “Wedding Party” and “The Anniversary Game” in 1969, appearing on CTV and CBC, as well as making guest appearances in variety shows. His popularity as “Canada’s leading TV talk show host” in the late 1970s significantly boosted his net worth. His hosting of “The Alan Hamel Show” on CTV from 1976 to 1980 garnered widespread popularity, and he also participated in other shows such as “Mantrap”, “Alan Hamel’s Comedy Bag”, and “Tattletales” on CBS in 1977.

    During this period, he began appearing as a commercial pitchman for American Stores, Alpha Beta Stores, and Acme Markets in various parts of the United States. Alan also ventured into production work and occasional acting. He also achieved success in business, selling the Thighmaster and founding SCL Industries, a manufacturing company that markets to countries worldwide.

    On the personal front, Alan was married to Marilyn Hanek and had two children before their marriage ended in 1977. He then married actress Suzanne Somers in 1977, and the couple has been known for their dancing. Suzanne has also been a contestant on “Dancing with the Stars”.
