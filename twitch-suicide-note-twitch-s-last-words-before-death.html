    Twitch Suicide Note:- Stephen “tWitch” Boss, was a freestyle hip-hop dancer, choreographer, actor, TV producer, and TV personality from the United States. On the American edition of So You Think You Can Dance in 2008, he came in second. He served as a co-host and co-executive producer of The Ellen DeGeneres Show from 2014 until the program’s termination in 2022.

    Twitch Suicide Note, tWitch’s Last Words Before Death

    Twitch left a note before his death due to a gunshot wound to the head. Law enforcement sources told TMZ that a note was left where Twitch died and that note hinted at challenges he faced in the past.

    Even though the content of his suicide note is not revealed yet but the investigators told that he committed suicide.

    Twitch’s grandfather Eddy Boss had a conversation two days ago with Twitch. Eddy told The Daily Mail on 15 December 2022,

    “We spoke as recently as Saturday on more than one occasion. He was the same happy-go-lucky person that he’s always been. We had no indication that anything was out of the ordinary.”

    “He was a very humble, generous person. He brought light into everybody’s lives. We’re completely devastated right now. All I can say is he was a beautiful person that contributed so much to this world and to everyone he met in a positive way. We miss him dearly and we’re hurting tremendously.”

    Eddy also said that Twitch sent a text message to Eddy before he committed suicide and Twitch wrote,

    “I love you Dad-Dad.” Our question is why? Where did it come from?”

    Twitch Passed away at 40

    The longtime DJ on Ellen DeGeneres’ talk show, Stephen “tWitch” Boss, passed away on Wednesday, according to his wife. He was 40. Boss was declared dead in Encino on Tuesday just before 11:30 a.m. local time, according to a representative for the Los Angeles County Medical Examiner-Coroner. According to online records, the deceased died by suicide, and the site of death was listed as a “hotel/motel.”

    His Wife, Allison Holker Boss, released a statement following the passing of her husband. She stated, “I have to share that my husband Stephen has left us with the hardest of hearts.” “He served as the foundation of our family, was the best husband and father, and served as an example to his followers. It would be an understatement to say he left a legacy, and his beneficial influence will endure.”

    Family, friends, and the community are important to her husband, according to his wife Holker Boss, who also stated that “leading with love and light meant everything to him.” She stated, “I am confident there won’t be a day that goes by that we won’t respect his memory. The family is requesting privacy. “Stephen, you are loved and missed.”

    According to Deadline, Boss appeared as a guest DJ on “The Ellen DeGeneres Show” in 2014. In 2020, he was appointed executive producer of the program.
