    Georgia football finally plays like the No. 1 team in win against Kentucky

    ATHENS, Ga. — There was something different in this game. Brock Bowers, maybe the most unassuming star player in Georgia football history, actually flexed in the end zone after a touchdown. Carson Beck, also not known for trash-talking, appeared to be doing that with a few Kentucky players.

    There was just a sense one got this week that Georgia was tired of not playing up to its usual standard, tired of hearing about its slow starts. Throw in an unbeaten opponent and a night game at home, and the elements were there for Georgia to finally show up and play a complete game.

    Thanks mainly to Beck and the offense, that’s precisely what happened. Georgia was electric on offense, scoring on eight of its first nine drives, including all six in the first half. The Bulldogs made a game of ranked opponents look like a guarantee game: No. 1 Georgia 51, No. 20 Kentucky 13.

    We may have a dynasty on our hands

    This is the Georgia team that can win a third straight national title. A complete, physical team. A dominant team. It looked like an angry team. One that had heard enough about its slow starts and struggles in its first two SEC games (including from me). With Alabama in charge in the SEC West and Georgia in charge in the SEC East, it looks more normal than we expected in the nation's best conference.

    Big day for Georgia's offense

    Georgia goes over 600 yards and 50 points with the backup offense in against Kentucky.

    It’s a no-silver-lining night for Mark Stoops. Meanwhile, Louisville just extended its lead to 24-13 over Notre Dame.

    Georgia up big at the half

    The story for Kentucky is that it was not prepared to meet this moment. The defense, typically one of the best in the country under Mark Stoops and coordinator Brad White, gave up a too-easy touchdown to open the game and never even really bothered Georgia in the first half. Giving up 384 yards and 34 points without a single stop in the first 30 minutes is a shocking level of incompetence, highlighted by twice leaving all-world tight end Brock Bowers running wide open.

    An even worse look? Offensive lineman Jager Burton killed both Kentucky’s first two promising drives with bone-headed penalties — a completely unnecessary hold and a stunning elbow drop to a defender who was already on the ground — and defensive lineman Deone Walker extending a UGA drive with an equally foolish personal foul. Combine those mistakes with yet another series of misfires by quarterback Devin Leary and the Wildcats were sunk by the end of the first quarter.

    Losing to the two-time defending national champion, a team you’ve lost to 13 straight times, is no great shame or shock, but to lose this way with a chance to make a national statement is surely going to sting Stoops and a fan base that wanted to believe this year was different.

    Halftime analysis as Georgia leads Kentucky, 34-7

    Well, everyone was wondering when Georgia would start faster and play a complete game. This is that game, and there were hints around the program this week that would happen. Just a sense that it was fed up with not playing to its potential, as well as the outside (and justified) criticism that it wasn't playing up to the usual Georgia standard. The fact it was an unbeaten opponent and a home night game added to the educated guess this would be the game the old Georgia finally showed up.

    Or the new Georgia, carried by its offense, especially Carson Beck, who passed for 307 yards in the first half alone. Georgia scored on all six of its possessions, and the two field goals came after Brock Bowers dropped what would have been a touchdown, and when the offense ran out of time at the end of the half. Just a total domination by the Georgia offense.
