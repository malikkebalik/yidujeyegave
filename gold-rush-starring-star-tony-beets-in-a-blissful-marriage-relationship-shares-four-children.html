    Tony Beets is a Dutch-Canadian gold miner and producer of Klondike gold rush. Beets is widely known for his appearance in the reality television show titled Gold Rush aired via Discovery Channel.

    Beets was born on 15th December 1959 in the Netherlands. Born to her Dutch parents, he is of Caucasian ethnicity and holds Dutch-Canadian Nationality.

    Blissful Marital Life with Minnie Beets

    The famous gold miner and television personality is a married man. Tony exchanged his wedding vows with Minnie Beets in 1984. The couple dated during their 20's before nuptials.

    The lovely couple shares four children; two of them are daughters, Monica Beets, Bianca Beets, and the rest of them are sons, Mike Beets and Kevin Beets. Since the wedding, Tony and his spouse are living happily. 

    How Much Does Beets Earn?

    The famous television personality and gold miner accumulate massive wealth from his profession. To date, Tony has made his appearance in numerous seasons of Gold Rush, which airs via Discovery Channel. 

    As per some sources, the average salary of a crew member on the Gold Rush is $100,000 per episode or they can even earn $2 Million per season, depends upon the condition. Hence, we can assume the remuneration of Mr Beets from a single episode. Reportedly, Tony's total net worth is estimated to be $15 million. 

    Besides, the famous television personality is living a lavish life. Beets owns a red Mercedes Convertible car (worth $145,000). He also holds a big floating industrial bucket (worth $1 million), which he bought during the 5th season of Gold Rush. 

    Started his career as a Gold Miner

    The Gold Rush star first moved to Whitehorse and started working as a gold miner. Later, he was transferred to milling machines for British Columbia (B.C) for the oil pipelines for Alberta. During the time, he made a breakthrough career in Yukon gold mine.

    Additionally, Tony's daughter Monica Beets is one of the significant crew of the show, as she began working with her father since her early days making her own money.
