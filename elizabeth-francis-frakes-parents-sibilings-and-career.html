    Based in Portland and Los Angeles, Elizabeth Francis Frakes, also known as Eliza Frakes, is a performer, writer, and filmmaker for the stage. Her mother, Genie Francis, is most recognized for her appearances as Genevieve Atkinson on “The Young and the Restless,” Diana Colville on “Days of Our Lives,” and Laura Spencer on “General Hospital.”

    On the other side, Frakes’ father, Jonathan, is well-known for playing Commander William Riker in the TV show Star Trek: The Next Generation as well as numerous motion pictures and television shows. In addition, he provided the voice of David Xanatos in the Disney television series Gargoyles and presented the anthology series Beyond Belief: Fact or Fiction.

    Eliza, the daughter of Genie and Jonathan, writes poetry, novels, screenplays, and plays. Her work primarily explores the socio-political environment, the function of comedy in theater, and the existence of absurdity in the everyday.

    Birth and Sibling of Elizabeth Francis Frakes Elizabeth Francis Frakes, the daughter of Jonathan and Genie, was born in Maine on May 30, 1997. The now 24-year-old Frakes and her brother, Jameson Ivor Frakes, grew up in Belfast, Maine.

    Elizabeth’s Theater Background Works

    Following her graduation from the Portland Experimental Theatre Ensemble, Eliza began her career as an instructor and lived and worked across the nation in writers’ rooms, theaters, sets, and other venues.

    She was a remote script covering writer for Fuzzy Door Productions while she was living in Los Angeles. Mash in Stupid F*$king Bird, Tabby in Cuddles, Lily in Waterlily, and Sarah in the short film Murky are just a few of Frake’s theater credits.

    Thus far, Frakes has written theater pieces on Remembrance Day and Moleman.

    She also belongs to Corrib Theatre.

    In September 2019, Frakes resumed her career outside of theater when she was hired as a writer’s assistant on a new show for CBS All Access. This allowed her to work with support staff and gain experience by sitting in the TV writers’ room.

    Eliza then concluded a year-long residency with Echo Theatre in 2020 as one of their chosen young playwrights, during which she made her stage debut with Moleman, her first full-length play.

    Frakes established the multidisciplinary art collective Might Be A Bad One Collective during this period. It is said that the group meets twice a month to create an environment that fosters creativity, accountability, and teamwork.

    Elizabeth Francis Frakes Taught Yoga and Wrote a Children’s Book.
