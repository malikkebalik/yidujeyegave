    Felicia Pearson’s Rise to Fame

    Felicia “Snoop” Pearson, an actress, rap artist, and author, has a net worth of $50 thousand dollars. Pearson had a difficult upbringing, living with a foster family and turning to drug dealing. She was convicted of second-degree murder at the age of fourteen and spent six and a half years in prison, where she earned her GED. Pearson met Michael K. Williams, who played Omar Little on “The Wire”, while hanging out in a club in Baltimore. Williams introduced her to the writers and producers of the series, and Pearson began appearing on the show as the character, Felicia “Snoop” Pearson. Pearson was also featured on the track, “It’s A Stick Up”, and wrote the memoir, “Grace After Midnight”, about her early childhood experiences.
